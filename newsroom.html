Cost centres or business drivers? Reimagining the role of GCCs in 2023

As a home to approximately 1,700 Global Capability Centres (GCCs), India has become the global hotspot for GCC expansions. A recent report by NASSCOM and Zinnov on the GCC India landscape says, the country will likely grow to a USD 58-61 Billion market by FY2025, with 1,900-2,000+ GCCs. This will have a direct impact on not only the country’s employment which is estimated to reach 1.8 to 2 million+ in FY2025. On the other hand, this could bring the tech ecosystem much closer, thus impacting the quality of education and training of the new-age workforce, and reskilling of existing talent.

Reimagining omni-channel retail with emerging tech

In the last decade, innovation in retail tech has piqued the interest and curiosity of businesses and consumers alike. We are moving fast towards an omni-channel future where retailers and customers can meet wherever they want to and get a consistent and seamless experience. It is important for businesses to build and optimize every channel in a way that gives customers both – the immersive experience of a retail store and the convenience of e-commerce. This holds especially true for retailers of consumer goods such as fashion, lifestyle, luxury and home improvement.

What GCCs need to do to thrive in the phygital world

Many Global Capability Centers (GCCs) began their migration to a new normal following the pandemic in 2021, based on more vigorous support for the GCC model, expanded functions beyond conventional workstreams, and increased use of digital technology. However, with the need to recalibrate strategies with the changing retail ecosystem, GCCs will need to keep up with the technological advancements changing dynamics, and the blurring lines between the physical and digital world.
